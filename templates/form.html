Pregnancy

During pregnancy, your placenta makes hormones that cause glucose to build up in your blood. Usually, your pancreas can send out enough insulin to handle it. But if your body can't make enough insulin or stops using insulin as it should, your blood sugar levels rise, and you get gestational diabetes.

A normal pregnancy lasts for about 280 days or 40 weeks.

Insulin Level

Insulin is a hormone that lowers the level of glucose (a type of sugar) in the blood.When blood glucose, also called blood sugar, levels rise after you eat, your pancreas releases insulin into the blood. Insulin then lowers blood glucose to keep it in the normal range.

A blood sugar level less than 140 mg/dL is normal. A reading of more than 200 mg/dL after two hours indicates diabetes.

Blood Pressure

Blood pressure is the pressure of blood pushing against the walls of your arteries. Having hypertension appears to increase the risk of type 2 diabetes, and having type 2 diabetes increases the risk of hypertension.

In fact, a person with diabetes and high blood pressure is four times as likely to develop heart disease than someone who does not have either of the conditions.

Glucose level

A blood glucose test is a blood test that screens for diabetes by measuring the level of glucose (sugar) in a person’s blood.The amount of glucose (“sugar”, measured in mg/dL) in your blood changes throughout the day and night.

A blood sugar level less than 140 mg/dL (7.8 mmol/L) is normal. A reading of more than 200 mg/dL (11.1 mmol/L) after two hours indicates diabetes.

BMI

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a person’s weight in kilograms divided by the square of height in meters. A high BMI can indicate high body fatness.

Being overweight (BMI of 25-29.9), or affected by obesity (BMI of 30-39.9) or morbid obesity (BMI of 40 or greater), greatly increases your risk of developing type 2 diabetes. The more excess weight you have, the more resistant your muscle and tissue cells become to your own insulin hormone.
